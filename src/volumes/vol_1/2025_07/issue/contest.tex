\headline{{\textbf{\Large\sffamily The Top Chop:}}\\
          {\textbf{\large\sffamily The Tree of the Month Contest}}}
\label{2025-07-contest}
\noindent
The July meeting of Twickenham Bonsai Club was a rugged affair, as members tackled the dramatic theme: \textit{``Root on Rock or Slab Planting''}.
From windswept cliffs to forested outcrops, each tree showcased nature's struggle and artistry.
Many entries brought their own stone or slate to life---a true celebration of the tenacity of roots.

For newcomers: each member may enter one tree monthly (novices may enter more, but only the best counts).
Entries are judged by peers---excluding owners---across four categories: trunk/branch, foliage, pot, and theme presentation.
Each judge can award up to 12 points.

\topic{Tree of the Month}
\noindent 
This month's display was a thrilling tribute to wild landscapes and daring roots.

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Chris Durne}'s Chinese Juniper (\textit{`Juniperus Chinensis'}, 140 points) --- A stunning root\--on\--rock composition; old bark and dense foliage accentuate the dramatic cling to a jagged cliff.

    \item \textbf{Tina Todd}'s Ferris Newton (\textit{`Ferris Newton'}, 133 points) --- A mysterious tree rarely seen in UK collections, presented in a natural slab with mossy ground and twisting nebari.

    \item \textbf{David Fishwick}'s Beech (\textit{`Fagus Sylvatica'}, 130 points) --- A graceful cascade from a granite slab, its foliage light and layered like a waterfall of green.

    \item \textbf{Steve Pallmer}'s Picea Glauca (\textit{`White Spruce'}, 121 points) --- A snowy alpine vibe with roots clenched to shale, full of crisp energy and subtle needle tone.

    \item \textbf{Mark Pittman}'s Hornbeam (\textit{`Carpinus Betulus'}, 114 points) --- A muscular trunk bursts from the edge of a flat stone, balancing mass and delicacy.

    \item \textbf{Tony Ulatowski}'s Chinese Elm (\textit{`Ulmus Parvifolia'}, 113 points) --- A powerful informal upright emerging from slate, its fine branching well\--balanced by smooth rock curves.

    \item \textbf{Grant Kinnaird}'s Hawthorn (\textit{`Crataegus Monogyna'}, 108 points) --- A gnarled, defiant form gripping a broken slab; white flowers lingering amongst dark foliage.

    \item \textbf{Brendan Gibbs}' Cotoneaster (\textit{`Cotoneaster Horizontalis'}, 104 points) --- A low, creeping tree clambering over stone with sprays of red berries and graceful movement.

    \item \textbf{Haidi Jenkin}'s Ash (\textit{`Fraxinus Excelsior'}, 96 points) --- Delicate compound leaves soften the strong vertical lines of the central rock; a poetic silhouette.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent 
\textbf{Chris Durne} claims this month's top prize with his dramatic \textit{Chinese Juniper} --- a brilliant example of roots defying gravity!

\topic{Novice Tree of the Month}
\noindent
Our novice category stepped up to the slab with bold creativity and confident hands.

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{John Mani}'s Japanese Elm (\textit{`Ulmus Davidiana Japonica'}, 113 points) --- A dynamic semi\--cascade rooted firmly into a rugged rock face, full of promise and presence.

    \item \textbf{Martin Redfern}'s Pyracantha (\textit{`Pyracantha Coccinea'}, 72 points) --- A graceful root\--on\--slab composition with orange berries peeking through glossy leaves.
\end{enumerate}

\textit{\textbf{Remember:} Novice members may present multiple trees, but only their highest\--scoring entry contributes to rankings.}

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{John Mani} takes this month's novice honour with his dramatic elm rooted in stone --- well done!
