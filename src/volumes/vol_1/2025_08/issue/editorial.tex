\byline{{\textbf{\Large\sffamily August Bonsai Club Meetup:}}\\
        {\textbf{\large\sffamily No Summer Slump Here: Critique, Cascades, and Curators}}}{The Newsletter Team}
\noindent
While August often brings a slower rhythm to bonsai work, our club's energy never dipped.
This month's meetup featured a special return: \textbf{Corin Tomlinson} of \href{https://www.bonsai.co.uk}{Greenwood Bonsai} led one of our most engaging workshops yet.
Blending sharp critique with lively discussion, Corin brought trees to life, pointing out subtle movement and proposing development plans that had members scribbling notes.
His breakdown of trunk structure sparked numerous ``aha!'' moments, all amid the group's enthusiastic banter.

\medskip
The \textbf{Tree of the Month} competition proved equally inspiring with the theme \textit{``Cascade and Semi\--Cascade''}.
From dramatic pines to delicate junipers, the entries sparked passionate debates about balance and naturalism.
The diversity of approaches was a testament to how even our \textit{``restful''} summer months can't stifle creativity.

\medskip
The momentum carried into the following day, when a select group enjoyed \textbf{Kew Gardens}' hospitality.
A curator Richard's behind\--the\--scenes tour revealed the secrets of display trees and the meticulous work in the growing\--on areas.
Between pruning demos and chats about soil, the team brainstormed 2026 club projects and bonded over well\--earned refreshments. 

\medskip
Looking ahead, \textbf{September 11th} promises another unforgettable evening as internationally renowned \textbf{Peter Warren} (\href{https://saruyama.co.uk}{Saruyama Bonsai}) joins us for what's sure to be a revelatory critique session.
With \textit{``Evergreens''} as the \textbf{Tree of the Month} theme, we anticipate particularly spirited discussions about needle density and winter silhouettes.
Whether you're a seasoned enthusiast or just bonsai\--curious, join us at the \textbf{Whitton Community Centre}---these are the nights that remind us why our club thrives year after year.
