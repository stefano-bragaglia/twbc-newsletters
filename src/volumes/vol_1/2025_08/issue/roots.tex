\headline{{\textbf{\Large\sffamily Bonsai Roots:}}\\ 
          {\textbf{\large\sffamily Meet the Members of TWBC}}}
\label{2025-08-roots}
\noindent
In every edition of \emph{Bonsai Roots,} we take a break from pruning and potting to spotlight the members who bring our club to life.
Each story adds a branch to the growing canopy of TWBC.
This month, we're delighted to introduce \textbf{Ryan Best!}

\begin{itemize} 
    \item \textbf{What's your name and where in the country do you live?}

    \emph{I'm Ryan Best, and I live in North West London.}

    \item \textbf{What are your favourite species of tree and why?}

    \emph{That changes all the time! At the moment it's a huge sweet chestnut on the grounds of a school where I work. It has all the features we try to recreate in bonsai. In my own collection, I mainly stick with native and naturalised species — they tend to be a little easier to care for.}

    \item \textbf{What aspect of bonsai do you enjoy?}

    \emph{I love finding new material, usually in gardens where a tree or shrub is due for removal. I'll grab whatever's at hand — a spade, a mattock, or even just a plastic bag — and make do. I've learned to be bold: wash the roots, flatten the base, and get the tree into soil as best I can.}

    \item \textbf{What aspects of bonsai do you find most challenging?}

    \emph{Honestly? Pretty much everything! Deciding on a front, planting angle, final image, or when to make a bold cut — it all feels challenging. Even basics like watering, soil mixes, pruning, or winter care can still trip me up. But I try not to stress too much; enjoying the process is more important.}

    \item \textbf{Who or what inspires you throughout your bonsai journey?}

    \emph{Mostly what I see on social media: great photos, videos of artists at work, and of course the posts from TWBC members. And always, the trees themselves.}
    
    \item \textbf{How long have you been interested in keeping bonsai?}

    \emph{About eight to ten years.}

    \item \textbf{What stimulated your interest in bonsai?}

    \emph{I've worked as a tree surgeon for 15 years and explored all sorts of related crafts — milling timber, carving, topiary, basketry, even spoon making. Bonsai just became the craft that stuck.}

    \item \textbf{If you could give someone new to bonsai any tips, what would it be?}

    \emph{I'm still collecting tips myself!}

    \item \textbf{Why do you enjoy being part of TWBC, and do you have any ideas or suggestions for the club's future?}

    \emph{The club has a wealth of knowledge and is so welcoming. It's active, friendly, and growing fast. No suggestions, but I'd love to take part more often.}

    \item \textbf{One word to describe what bonsai means to you?}

    \emph{Decompression.}

    \item \textbf{What are your future goals regarding your own trees and learning more about the hobby?}

    \emph{I'd like to get some of my trees in front of more experienced eyes, keep as many alive as I can, and just keep enjoying working on them.}

    \item \textbf{How would you like to see your bonsai journey progress in the future?}

    \emph{A few workshops would help. I'd like to get comfortable with carving, and one day feel like I have a couple of genuinely decent trees.}
\end{itemize}

It's been great to hear Ryan's bonsai journey — rooted in curiosity, boldness, and a love for trees.
Who will we meet next time?
Stay tuned for more in \emph{Bonsai Roots}!
