\byline{{\textbf{\Large\sffamily March Bonsai Club Meetup:}}\\
        {\textbf{\large\sffamily Shake Off the Old Soil: Time to Repot!}}}{The Newsletter Team}
\noindent
March's meeting saw another excellent turnout, with 18 members gathering to share their love of bonsai.
As always, the evening was filled with lively chatter and the gentle rustle of leaves as several members brought along their trees to work on, exchanging tips and techniques.

\medskip
The \textbf{Tree of the Month} and \textbf{Novice Tree of the Month} competitions were as inspiring as ever, with this month's theme, \textit{``Yamadori, Urban or Wild,''} drawing a range of fascinating entries.
It was a joy to see the creativity and skill on display, as members showcased trees with unique stories and character.

\medskip
The highlight of the evening was \textbf{Chris Durne's workshop}, which offered a fresh perspective on bonsai planning.
Chris guided us through sketching our trees as they are now and envisioning their development in 5 and 10 years' time.
This exercise encouraged us to think strategically about the long\--term training and shaping of our trees, sparking plenty of ideas.

\medskip
With the \textbf{bonsai show season} underway, excitement is building for next weekend's \textbf{Newark Bonsai-Fest}, replacing the former Doncaster Bonsai Experience.
A club delegation will be attending, and we're looking forward to the displays, inspiration, and perhaps a treasure or two.

\medskip
A big thank you to everyone who attended and contributed to another enjoyable evening.
We're already looking forward to next month's meeting and hope to see you all there!
