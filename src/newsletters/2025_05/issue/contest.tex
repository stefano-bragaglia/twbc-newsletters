The May meeting of Twickenham Bonsai Club bloomed with colour as members embraced the theme: \textit{``Any Flowering Tree''}. From cotoneasters to azaleas, the display celebrated spring in full. Tiny \textit{Mame} trees (under 6 inches) also made a guest appearance---un\-judged but delightful. 
% Photos follow the contest gallery.

For newcomers: each member may enter one tree monthly (novices may enter more, but only the best counts). Entries are judged by peers---excluding owners---across four categories: trunk/branch, foliage, pot, and theme presentation. Each judge can award up to 12 points.

\topic{Tree of the Month}
\noindent 
% This month's floral showcase saw breathtaking interpretations of flowering species, each tree a petalled sonnet to the season. Results are ordered by points received:
This month's showcase featured stunning flowering trees---each one a tribute to the season.

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{David Fishwick}'s cotoneaster (\textit{`Cotoneaster Horizontalis'}, 107 points) --- A powerful \textit{yamadori} with carved apex, a few red berries and pink flowers about to bloom.
    
    \item \textbf{Chris Durne}'s cotoneaster (\textit{`Cotoneaster Horizontalis'}, 91 points) --- A windswept tree, with a great line, white flowers and red berries in an unusual strong pot.
    
    \item \textbf{Brendan Gibbs}' cotoneaster (\textit{`Cotoneaster Horizontalis'}, 82 points) --- An sleek \textit{cascade} in a tall vase, whose deep green foliage is speckled with white flowers.

    \item \textbf{Mark Pittman}'s Wisteria (\textit{`Wisteria Sinensis'}, 72 points) --- A charming informal upright with lush fresh foliage and lovely cascades of pale violet flowers.
    
    \item \textbf{Terry Wilson}'s Potentilla (\textit{`Potentilla Fruticosa'}, 69 points) --- A complex \textit{literati} balancing deadwood and live vein with a crown of tiny leaves and pale flowers.

    \item \textbf{Edward Mason}'s American cranberry\-bush (\textit{`Viburnum Trilobum Marshall'}, 67 points) --- A leaning delicate tree shading a skull towering on the wanderers below.

    \item \textbf{Tina Todd}'s spindle (\textit{`Euonymus Europaeus'}, 66 points) --- A well presented \textit{kabudachi} with little yellow flowers and unique crested branches.

    \item \textbf{Haidi Jenkin}'s Satsuki azalea (\textit{`Azalea Japonica'}, 52 points) --- A stunning little tree, with a wide canopy mirroring the pot and lovely delicate white and pink flowers.

\end{enumerate}

\noindent 
\textbf{David Fishwick}'s claims this month's honours with his \textit{`Cotoneaster Horizontalis'} --- congratulations!

\topic{Novice Tree of the Month}
\noindent
% Our novice category bloomed with promise this month, demonstrating how new talent flourishes like spring buds:
Our novice entries showed great promise, budding with fresh talent.

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Martin Redfern}'s cotoneaster (\textit{`Cotoneaster Horizontalis'}, 66 points) --- An imposing \textit{semi\--cascade} with emerald green leaves and and pale pink flowers.
    
    \item \textbf{Stefano Bragaglia}'s Satsuki azalea (\textit{`Azalea Japonica'}, 58 points) --- A delicate \textit{kabudachi} with pale blossoms.

    \item \textbf{Caroline Mann}'s Satsuki azalea (\textit{`Azalea Japonica'}, 53 points) --- A flowing tree with pale yellow blossoms.
    
    \item \textbf{Laura Shantonas}' Lily\--of\--the\--Valley (\textit{`Pieris Japonica'}, 50 points) --- A charming twin trunk with delicate new leaves and delicate flowers.

    \item \textbf{Riz Mirza}'s Satsuki azalea (\textit{`Azalea Japonica'}, 30 points) --- A slanted elegant tree with crimson flowers.    
\end{enumerate}
