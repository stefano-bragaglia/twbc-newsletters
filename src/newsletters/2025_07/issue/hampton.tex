\byline{{\textbf{\Large\sffamily Bonsai Shows:}}\\
        {\textbf{\large\sffamily Hampton Court Palace Garden Festival}}}{Chris Durne}
\label{2025-07-show}
\noindent
\textit{2nd–7th July 2025} \\
\textit{Hampton Court Palace, Surrey. KT8 9AU.}

\medskip
The Hampton Court Palace Garden Festival once again provided a wonderful stage for the art of bonsai, drawing tens of thousands of visitors to the iconic grounds of Hampton Court Palace.
Organised by the Royal Horticultural Society (RHS), this major regional flower show is a venue where commercial bonsai sellers exhibit each year, and offers a valuable opportunity for amateur enthusiasts to demonstrate that we, too, can create displays of the highest quality.
It is also a fantastic platform to promote \emph{Twickenham Bonsai Society} as a local club --- helping to raise our profile, engage with the public, and potentially welcome new members into our community.

\medskip
Bonsai has long been a welcome presence at Hampton Court, consistently captivating visitors with its blend of craftsmanship, patience, and natural beauty.
Throughout the week, members from various societies actively engaged with the public, answering the usual questions --- most often, \emph{``How old is that tree?''}
While this is usually difficult to answer with certainty, there was one clear exception: Nick Payne's Chinese Juniper, which he grew from seed and knows to be exactly 40 years old!
Meanwhile, styled trees on loan from Driftwood Bonsai and prepared by me invited further interaction and interest from festival attendees.

\medskip
\topic{The Bonsai Exhibitors}
\noindent
Three main exhibitors led the bonsai presence at the festival:

\medskip
\textbf{Derbyshire Bonsai} --- A respected name on the UK bonsai show circuit, Derbyshire Bonsai delivered a clean, professional, and carefully curated display.
Their consistent participation over the years has helped to raise awareness of bonsai in the broader horticultural world.
For any follow\--up or contact, David Nicholos serves as their main representative.

\medskip
\textbf{Driftwood Bonsai} --- With a reputation for championing sustainable and environmentally conscious bonsai practices, Driftwood Bonsai once again impressed with a thoughtful and modern exhibit.
Their approach highlights the ecological potential of bonsai cultivation.
The group's contact is Mark Whitworth.

\medskip
\textbf{UK Bonsai Association (UKBA) Collective} --- Arguably the centrepiece of this year's bonsai showcase, the UKBA Collective united artists and clubs from across the UK to produce a collaborative display of exceptional quality.
Their efforts culminated in a historic achievement: the awarding of the prestigious \textbf{Tudor Rose Award}.

\medskip
\topic{The Tudor Rose Award}
\noindent
The \textbf{Tudor Rose Award} is the highest honour given at the RHS Hampton Court Palace Garden Festival --- akin to \textit{``Best in Show.''}
This award recognises not only aesthetic excellence but also innovation, craftsmanship, and overall impact.
For the first time in history, a bonsai exhibit received this distinction.
The award, which includes a gold\--threaded emblem hand\--stitched by the Embroidery Guild based at Hampton Court, was presented by Show Manager Helen Boem.

Mark and Mingchen Moreland, leading figures behind the exhibit, were also presented with a \textbf{12\--point Gold award} and the \textbf{Best in the Floral Marquee Award}, an achievement made even more remarkable given the competition from over 70 commercial plant nurseries.
The RHS judging panel commented that they \textit{``could have stood all day and wondered at the quality of the bonsai.''}
Their contribution marks a landmark moment not just for UKBA but for the entire UK bonsai community.

\medskip
\topic{Context and Additional Achievements}
\noindent
This accolade at Hampton Court follows another major triumph earlier in the season at the RHS Chelsea Flower Show --- considered the premier floral event globally --- where the Federation of British Bonsai Societies, with essential support from the Bonsai Boys, earned a Gold Medal.
While Chelsea remains unmatched in prestige, Hampton Court stands as a vital regional platform.
The UKBA, again supported by the Bonsai Boys, took the lead in coordinating the Hampton Court display.

\medskip
\topic{Twickenham Bonsai Society's Involvement}
\noindent
Under the UKBA banner, the Bonsai Boys extended an invitation to members of Twickenham Bonsai Society to participate in the exhibit's build and presentation.
Those able to assist included Tony Ulatowski, Nick Lloyd, Liz Panton, and myself.
TWBC's involvement at both Hampton Court and Chelsea has been deeply rewarding.
To achieve top honours at both festivals within the same season is a rare accomplishment, showcasing the strength of collaboration, skill, and dedication within the UK bonsai scene.

\medskip
\topic{Thanks to the Community}
\noindent
A heartfelt thank you is extended to everyone who contributed to bonsai's remarkable presence at Hampton Court this year --- organisers, exhibitors, and the many volunteers who gave their time and energy to ensure its success.

Special recognition goes to the UKBA members, including several from our own club, who supported the project in countless ways --- from setup and logistics to public engagement and coordination. 
% This event has again shown how strong, united, and passionate the UK bonsai community can be. 

Particular thanks are due to Mark and Mingchen Moreland, whose leadership and commitment helped deliver a historic first for UKBA.
Their work has made us all proud, and we echo the sentiment expressed by the RHS judging panel: the quality of the exhibit was truly worthy of wonder.

That said, it must be acknowledged that preparing for two major shows back\--to\--back has taken its toll on the current team.
At present, there are no plans for the same group to return next year.
However, if members of Twickenham Bonsai Society are willing to take up the mantle, the opportunity to show at the world's leading flower shows remains open.

\medskip
\hfill\textit{Chris Durne}
