\headline{{\textbf{\Large\sffamily The Top Chop:}}\\
          {\textbf{\large\sffamily The Tree of the Month Competition}}}
\label{2025-11-contest}
\noindent
November's meeting brought a sense of reflection and rediscovery to our benches, as members revisited trees from previous competitions this year.
The theme, \textit{``Any Tree Previously Submitted''}, invited artists to re\--examine their work, refine details, and explore new interpretations.
The collection was a delight to behold: familiar forms, presented with renewed care, featured subtle changes in foliage, structure, or seasonal character, offering a fresh perspective.
Quiet appreciation mingled with gentle conversation, admiring the skill and creativity evident.

If you are new to the Tree of the Month competition, here is a short summary of the rules.
Novice members may enter any number of trees each month, but only their highest\--scoring entry contributes to their monthly ranking.
General members are limited to a single entry per month.
To be eligible, all trees must have been owned for at least one year, and no tree may be entered for competition more than twice in a single year.

General members must adhere to the monthly theme\iffalse (see p.~\pageref{2025-11-themes})\fi, while novice members are encouraged, but not required, to follow it.
All attending members are encouraged to vote on every tree except their own.
Each member may award between 1 (lowest) and 3 (highest) points for each of 4 categories: trunk and branch structure, foliage quality, pot suitability, and overall presentation and interpretation of the theme, giving a total score of 4 to 12 points per tree.
Please ensure you cast votes for all trees presented, to provide a fair distribution of points.

All valid submissions earn 5 submission points.
Trees placed in the top eight positions receive 5 entry points plus additional positioning points based on their ranking: 15 for 1st, down by 2 per position till the 8th.
Rankings are determined by totalling the points awarded to each tree.
For further details, please refer to the full rules at p.~\pageref{2025-11-rules}.

\topic{General Tree of the Month}
\noindent
This month's General category showcased a remarkable array of previously submitted trees, each revisited with care, demonstrating both the artists' skill and their evolving vision.

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Tina Todd}'s ``Ferris Newton'' Box (\textit{`Buxus sempervirens ``Ferris Newton'''}, 138 points) --- An idyllic composition dominated by a raft forest on a hand\--made slab.
    \item \textbf{Brendan Gibbs}' ``Ferris Newton'' Box (\textit{`Buxus sempervirens ``Ferris Newton'''}, 119 points) --- A captivating tree with exposed roots, contorted jins and nice canopy.
    \item \textbf{David Fishwick}'s Hawthorn (\textit{`Crataegus Monogyna'}, 113 points) --- A leaning tree with a gentle line and an imposing trunk.
    \item \textbf{Steve Palmer}'s ``Ferris Newton'' Box (\textit{`Buxus sempervirens ``Ferris Newton'''}, 100 points) --- Another compact tree in a hand\--made pot reminding of a gentle slope.
    \item \textbf{Terry Wilson}'s Firethorn Pyracantha (\textit{`Pyracantha coccinea'}, 93 points) --- An engaging tree in a lovely pot, covered in fruits.
    \item \textbf{Grant Kinnaird}'s Welsh Larch (\textit{`Larix Decidua'}, 87 points) --- A small tree with a lot of character recreating a peaceful and colourful image.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent 
\textbf{Tina Todds}' Box claims this month's honours with her bucolic \textit{`Ferris Newton Box'} --- congratulations!

\medskip
\topic{Novice Tree of the Month}
\noindent
In the Novice category, members embraced the opportunity to refine and re\--present their earlier entries, revealing growing confidence and a keen eye for detail in every tree displayed.

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Victoria Miquel}'s Atlantic blue cedar (\textit{`Cedrus Atlantica ``Glauca'''}, 88 points) --- An elegant literati with lots of character.
    \item \textbf{Martin Redfern}'s Cotoneaster (\textit{`Cotoneaster Horizontalis'}, 68 points) --- A little captivating semi\--cascade mame complemented by a nice deep blue hexagonal pot.
    \item \textbf{John Mari}'s Chinese Elm (\textit{`Ulmus Parviflora'}, 48 points) --- A charming broom style shohin tree in full winter display.
    \item \textbf{Garry Nichols}' Hawthorn (\textit{`Crataegus Monogyna'}, 40 points) --- A dramatic wind\--swept tree in training.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent
Our warmest congratulations go to \textbf{Victoria Miquel}, whose graceful Blue Cedar claimed top honours this month.

Until then --- keep refining, keep enjoying the journey, and may your bonsai bring a little extra cheer to the season.
